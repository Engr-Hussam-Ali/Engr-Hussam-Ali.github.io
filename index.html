Project 01

Tilt Sensor PCB Assembly — FEA Optimization & Mesh Convergence

Minimizing MEMS deformation through screw-position optimization and rigorous mesh convergence analysis in Abaqus/Standard

Problem Statement

A MEMS-based tilt sensor mounted on a multi-layer PCB assembly experiences parasitic rotational deformation due to thermomechanical loading. The deformation at the MEMS center degrades measurement accuracy. The goal: find the optimal screw mounting positions on the large PCB to minimize MEMS rotational displacement (UR1, UR2, UR3).

Assembly

Multi-component assembly: Large PCB (FR-4, C3D8R), Small PCB (FR-4, S4R shells), MEMS sensor (EMC, S4R), ABS housings (C3D10), steel screw cylinders (C3D8R), LoRa module, MCU, and PCB Plus — connected through 20 tie constraints with a critical load path through ABS housing.

Load path: Boundary conditions flow through the ABS housing and screws to the large PCB, then to the small PCB and finally the MEMS sensor.

Methodology

1

Parametric DOE — 100 FEA Models

Generated 100 design configurations by varying the X/Y positions of three mounting screws on the large PCB. Each configuration was solved in Abaqus/Standard under identical thermal-structural loading. Rotational displacements (UR1, UR2, UR3) at the MEMS center node were extracted as response quantities.

2

Surrogate Model Construction

Built a response surface surrogate from the 100-point dataset to map screw positions → MEMS deformation. This enabled rapid evaluation of the full design space and identification of the optimal screw configuration that minimizes parasitic MEMS rotation, improving tilt measurement accuracy.

3

Mesh Convergence Study — Systematic Debugging

Performed a rigorous mesh convergence analysis on the optimized configuration. Discovered that node-based constraint sets caused silent failures after remeshing — sign reversals and non-physical jumps in results. Diagnosed and resolved through three systematic fixes:

FIX 1 — Replaced node-based cylinder master sets with element-based surfaces + surface-to-surface discretization
FIX 2 — Replaced node-based MEMS tie sets with geometry-based element surfaces for shell–shell coupling
FIX 3 — Geometrically partitioned the large PCB at cylinder footprints, creating dedicated pad surfaces for stable tie behavior across mesh sizes

Key Engineering Insights

  • → Node-based sets are unreliable across remeshing — node IDs change completely, causing silent constraint failures
  • → Geometric partitioning at contact footprints is essential for tie stability across mesh refinement levels
  • → Isolation runs (refining one component while holding others fixed) are the correct diagnostic methodology
  • → Gradual UR2 drift across refinement steps signals tie surface instability rather than mesh density insufficiency
